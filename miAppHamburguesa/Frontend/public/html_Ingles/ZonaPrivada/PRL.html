Recruiting staff

Types: Employment contracts, temporary contracts, part-time contracts, permanent contracts, training and apprenticeship contracts, contracts for the insertion of people with disabilities, among others.

Deadlines: Depends on the type of contract and the labor regulations in force in your country.

Employee Enrollment

Deadline: Employee enrollment must be done before they start working at the restaurant. This may vary according to local labor legislation.

Workplace Hazards

Waiter

Physical strain due to repetitive movements.

Risk of cuts and burns when handling hot and sharp objects.

Exposure to slips and falls, especially in service areas.

Cook

Risk of burns from handling hot utensils and equipment.

Exposure to cuts when handling knives and other sharp utensils.

Risk of food poisoning from food handling.

Cleaning Staff

Exposure to cleaning chemicals, with the risk of skin irritation or burns and respiratory tract irritation.

Risk of slips and falls when cleaning wet or slippery surfaces.

Possible exposure to sharp or cutting objects when handling trash or waste.

Work Accidents

Procedures: Immediate report of the accident to the competent authority and to the occupational risk prevention service. Investigation to determine the causes and establish corrective measures.

Actions to Take: Immediate medical assistance, monitoring of the health status of the affected worker, implementation of measures to prevent future similar accidents.

With or without medical leave: Depending on the severity of the accident, the worker may need medical leave for recovery. In less serious cases, corrective measures can be taken without the need for leave.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPEs)

Mandatory supply and use of PPEs such as gloves, aprons, caps, non-slip footwear, ear protectors, among others, according to the position and identified risks.

Collective Agreement

The collective agreement in our hamburger restaurant establishes the rights and duties of both the company and the workers. It includes aspects such as minimum wage, working hours, breaks, paid vacations, sick leave, paid leave, among others.

In addition, the collective agreement may contain additional improvements negotiated between management and worker representatives, such as bonuses, health insurance, daycare assistance, among others.

Legal Representation of Workers (LRW)

The legal representation of workers in our restaurant may be handled by unions or democratically elected representatives within the company. These representatives are responsible for safeguarding the interests of the workers, participating in labor negotiations, and collaborating in conflict resolution.

The advantages of having legal representation of workers include greater bargaining power and more effective protection of labor rights. The rights of legal representatives of workers include the right to be informed and consulted on decisions affecting workers, as well as to participate in the development of occupational safety and health policies and other workplace safety measures.

Selection Process

The selection process in our restaurant follows several steps to ensure the hiring of suitable personnel:

1. Job posting: The vacancy is advertised through various channels, specifying the job requirements.

2. Reception and analysis of resumes: Received resumes are reviewed to select candidates who best fit the desired profile.

3. Individual or group interviews: Interviews are conducted to assess technical skills and competencies required for the position.

4. Practical tests: In some cases, practical tests may be conducted to assess specific skills required for the position.

5. Reference checks: Work references are contacted to verify the candidate's experience and performance.

6. Final hiring decision: Based on the evaluation of candidates in relation to the criteria and competencies required for the position.
