Collective Bargaining

The Works Council can negotiate collective agreements with the restaurant management on behalf of the workers. This includes agreements on wages, working hours, working conditions, benefits, and other employment-related aspects.

Worker Representation

The Council acts as a representative of the workers' interests to the restaurant management. They can communicate employees' concerns, complaints, or suggestions and seek solutions to labor issues.

Occupational Health and Safety

The Works Council can collaborate in implementing occupational health and safety measures in the hamburger restaurant. This involves identifying workplace hazards, proposing preventive actions, and monitoring compliance with safety regulations.

Mediation and Conflict Resolution

In case of labor disputes, the Works Council can mediate between workers and management to find a satisfactory solution for both parties. This may include resolving disputes over working conditions, discrimination, harassment, among others.

Participation in Management

Depending on labor legislation and the restaurant's internal policy, the Works Council may have the right to participate in the company's decision-making on matters affecting workers, such as changes in work organization, hirings, dismissals, among others.

Information and Consultation

The Council may demand relevant information from the restaurant management on issues affecting workers, such as changes in company policies, strategic decisions, or economic situations.

Identification of the Applicable Collective Agreement for "Burger City" Burger Joint

  • Scope of the Collective Agreement:
    • Covered Worker Categories: Kitchen staff, customer service, cleaning, and administrative.
    • Geographic Area: City of "Burger City" and surrounding areas.
    • Included Industries or Sectors: Catering and hospitality.
  • Duration of the Agreement:
    • Validity: From January 1, 2024, to December 31, 2026.
    • Renegotiation: A notice period of 90 days before the expiration is established to initiate renewal negotiations.
  • Salaries and Benefits:
    • Minimum Wages: Minimum wage rates set by worker category according to current labor legislation.
    • Benefits: Performance bonuses, meal allowances, health insurance for full-time employees, and voluntary retirement plans.
  • Working Hours:
    • Normal Hours: 40 hours per week, distributed in rotating shifts according to operational needs.
    • Overtime: Paid according to legal regulations, with a maximum limit of 12 additional hours per week.
    • Breaks: 30 minutes break for every 4 hours worked.
    • Night Shifts: Additional compensation of 25% on the base salary for night work.
  • Working Conditions:
    • Leave: 15 days of annual vacation, sick leave in accordance with labor legislation, and parental leave as provided by law.
    • Work Safety: Food safety protocols and training in workplace risk prevention.
    • Workplace Adaptations: Procedures to request reasonable accommodations for employees with specific needs.
  • Dispute Resolution:
    • Complaint Procedures: Employees can file formal complaints with the human resources department.
    • Mediation: Internal mediation is encouraged to resolve disputes before resorting to external instances.
    • Arbitration: In case of persistent disagreement, a neutral arbitrator designated by both parties will be used.
  • Other Provisions:
    • Training and Development: Training opportunities in culinary skills, customer service, and leadership are offered.
    • Non-Discrimination: Non-discrimination policy based on gender, race, religion, sexual orientation, or any other protected category by law.
    • Specific Roles: Training programs for leadership roles and technical specialization are established.

This collective agreement provides a solid framework to ensure regulatory compliance and promote positive labor relations within "Burger City".
